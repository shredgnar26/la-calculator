⚡ LAST

Local Anesthetic Systemic Toxicity
⚠️ CALL FOR HELP IMMEDIATELY
Not a substitute for clinical judgment or institutional emergency protocols.
🧠 CNS — Early Signs ▼

Typically within 60 seconds of injection. CNS excitation usually precedes CV collapse — but bupivacaine can go straight to arrest.

Perioral / tongue numbness — often first symptom
Tinnitus / auditory changes
Metallic taste
Dizziness / lightheadedness
Visual disturbance — blurred, double vision
Agitation / anxiety / sudden behavioral change
Slurred speech
⚠️ With rapid IV injection or high-potency agents, early CNS signs may be absent. CV collapse can be the first presentation.
🧠 CNS — Severe ▼
Muscle twitching / tremors
Seizures — tonic-clonic
Loss of consciousness
Respiratory arrest
Seizures: Benzodiazepines first line — midazolam 0.05–0.1 mg/kg IV or lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg.
Low-dose propofol (20 mg increments) only if benzo unavailable AND patient hemodynamically stable.
Airway: 100% O₂, hyperventilate — acidosis potentiates toxicity.
❤️ Cardiovascular Signs ▼
Hypertension / tachycardia — early excitatory phase
Hypotension
Bradycardia
Conduction changes — PR prolongation, wide QRS, ST changes
Ventricular arrhythmias — VT, VF
Cardiac arrest
⛔ Avoid: Vasopressin, calcium channel blockers, beta-blockers, lidocaine/procainamide for arrhythmia.
Epinephrine: ≤1 mcg/kg IV only — large doses impair lipid rescue efficacy.
Amiodarone acceptable for refractory VT/VF.
Standard ACLS will fail without lipid emulsion. Standard epi doses and vasopressin worsen outcome.
🚨 Immediate Actions ▼
1STOP injection immediately
2Call for help — activate emergency response, get LAST rescue kit / Intralipid
3Airway — 100% O₂, secure airway, hyperventilate to correct acidosis
4IV access — large bore if not present
5Seizures — benzodiazepine (midazolam 0.05–0.1 mg/kg or lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV)
6Lipid emulsion NOW — 20% Intralipid, dose below ↓
7If arrest — modified ACLS: prolonged CPR, epi ≤1 mcg/kg, consider ECMO/cardiopulmonary bypass
8Monitor ≥ 2 hours after stabilization — delayed recurrence possible
⏱ Approximately 3-minute window from recognition to lipid administration before hypoxia + acidosis make cardiac blockade irreversible.
